Introduction

A good education is the foundation on which children and young adults build their lives and careers, with educational outcomes historically strongly tied to eventual life outcomes. In the rapidly evolving political, technological, and cultural landscapes of the modern world, education policy has been slow to adapt, and as a consequence many feel that the quality of American primary education is suffering. Many differing strategies for organizing and funding education at the national level in recent decades (most notably the Common Core and No Child Left Behind policies), but opinion is divided on the efficacy of these schema, and many feel that they were already outdated the moment they were rolled out.

It should come as no surprise, then, that research into educational outcomes and their causes has been a priority in academia and government for some time; as a consequence, significant sources of data are available to analyze the problems we find in education. This provides an opportunity for the use of machine learning techniques to answer questions related to modern education, as well as attempt to discern underlying patterns within the data that researchers may not be looking directly for.
